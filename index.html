Project Description

In this project we imagine that people now have access to a rapidly growing number of public data sources from the web/cloud which are getting augmented with personal sensors which now commonly talk to mobile phones or web pages. We look at designing an interface to make this kind of data and more available on your personal bathroom mirror (single person use), which could act as a large display touch display.

Project Sketches

Sketch 1

This is how the mirror starts and is at its default. It is complete with a Hide All button in the upper left corner, a light change button, a voice search button, and 5 "Lifestyle" buttons: Calendar, News and Messages, Clock, Weather, and Health (listed from top to bottom). A digital clock is also displayed in the upper right corner. Included are a speech button and a lighting button. The lighting button changes the color of the tint of the mirror. This is used for aesthetic purposes, typically.

Sketch 2

A low priority feature, the voice search allows users to play music or ask basic questions. A voice wave appears between the speech and light button to indicate the mirror is waiting for input.

Sketch 3

This feature allows users to hide all buttons in order to prioritize mirror usage.

Sketch 4

This sketch demonstrates the usage of the calendar feature. The calendar also displays up to two upcoming events. A back button is included to re-minimize the calendar. This back button is included in all tabs.

Sketch 5

This sketch demonstrates the usage of the news feed feature which also includes messages and social media notifications. All tabs also include a pop-out feature in order to open a corresponding application on the user's phone.

Sketch 6

This sketch demonstrates the usage of the clock tab. The clock tab includes a digital and analog clock and well as an alarm and timer feature. The alarm is set by selecting a time and hitting the SET button. Numbers change by pressing on the minute or hour digit, which increases it by 1. The timer is set the same way and comes with a run and stop button.

The weather feature comes with a large symbol that has the date and day with the current day's high, low, current temperature, and chance of rain displayed. Underneath, the high and lows of the week is listed along with a symbol to indicate rainy, cloudy, or sunny weather.

Sketch 8The final tab, the health tab, lists three seperate applications: the sleep tracker, activity and usage levels, and the weight tab.

Sketch 9

The first of the three health apps, the sleep app, displays a Sleep Tracker chart. The chart includes the current day's sleep as well as the sleep the user recorded the night before. The specific times in each stage of sleep is listed in a table underneath the graph. Also listed is the previous week's average for comparison.

Sketch 10

The activity levels app displays the amount of time the user spent in front of the mirror on the current day as well the previous day's readings. Another graph is displayed that lists the physical activity levels. Three measurements are included: the current days, the previous day, and the weekly average.

Sketch 11

Finally the last sketch shows the weight app. This app very simply displays the users current weight, last recorded weight on the day before, and the last week's avergae weight. At the bottom, the amount gained/lost is displayed.
